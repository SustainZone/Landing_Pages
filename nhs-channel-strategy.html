Entry Points
Three Specific Doors Into the NHS System

The NHS compliance landscape has three distinct mechanisms that create supplier demand for carbon accounting tools. Each requires a different relationship and a different ask.

Live Now · Mandatory
PPN 06/21

Procurement Policy Note 06/21

All suppliers bidding for central government contracts over £5m must submit a Carbon Reduction Plan covering Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions. This is already law. Thousands of NHS suppliers are either non-compliant or producing CRPs that lack Scope 3 rigour.

SustainZone's adaptive Scope 3 engine is precisely what CRP production requires — sector-specific, assumption-documented, confidence-scored. This is not a future opportunity. The burning platform is lit.

The ask: Position SustainZone explicitly as a "PPN 06/21 Scope 3 CRP tool." Build a one-page mapping of SustainZone outputs to CRP requirements. Use this as the anchor sales document for all NHS supplier conversations.
NHS-Specific · Escalating
NHS Supplier Roadmap

NHS Net Zero Supplier Roadmap

NHS England's Supplier Roadmap requires large suppliers (>£5m NHS spend) to report full Scope 1, 2, 3 and have a verified Net Zero plan. Medium suppliers (£1m–£5m) must report Scope 1+2 with a plan. Milestones escalate annually through 2030.

The Greener NHS Programme Office at NHS England owns this. A relationship with this team converts to formal endorsement language in the Roadmap guidance — which is updated annually and distributed to all suppliers.

The ask: Meet with Greener NHS Programme team. Offer to co-design the Scope 3 methodology guidance section of the 2026 Supplier Roadmap update. Recognition in that document is worth more than any paid campaign.
Procurement Enabler
G-Cloud / CCS Framework

Crown Commercial Service Listing

All NHS bodies procure software through G-Cloud — the Crown Commercial Service's cloud software framework. A G-Cloud listing (Lot 2: Cloud Software) makes SustainZone directly procurable by any NHS trust, ICB, or procurement body without a full tender process.

This is not how suppliers buy the tool — they buy direct. But G-Cloud listing makes SustainZone procurable for any NHS body that wants to co-fund supplier access as part of their own Scope 3 Category 1 improvement programme.

The ask: Apply for G-Cloud 14 listing under Lot 2. This is a prerequisite for any NHS body that wants to procure and distribute SustainZone access to their supplier base — which is exactly the buyer-funds-supplier model that unlocks the highest volume.

The Risk That Kills the Channel

The NHS system moves slowly, and recognition once given can be withdrawn. The single largest risk is receiving NHS Supply Chain toolkit listing before the methodology is independently validated — and then having an early client receive an audit challenge on their CRP that references SustainZone's output. One failed audit, covered in a trade association newsletter, poisons the whole channel. The NHS supplier community is small and well-networked. Reputational damage at this scale travels faster than any marketing campaign.

Rule: Do not seek NHS Supply Chain recognition until at least one SustainZone-produced CRP has been through a formal assurance review — by a Big 4 firm, a sustainability standard body, or an NHS-approved auditor — and passed. The reference clients are not just for the sales narrative. They are the methodology validation process.
