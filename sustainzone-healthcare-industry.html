The Framework

What Is the NHS Evergreen Assessment?

Launched in June 2023 by NHS England's Central Commercial Function and Greener NHS teams, the Evergreen Sustainable Supplier Assessment is the NHS's standardised route for suppliers to demonstrate their sustainability maturity — described as "one conversation with the NHS."

The NHS Net Zero Commitment

In October 2020, the NHS became the world's first national health system to commit to net zero.
Net zero by 2040 for direct emissions (Scope 1 & 2 — NHS Carbon Footprint).
Net zero by 2045 for the full supply chain (Scope 3 — NHS Carbon Footprint Plus).
The NHS supply chain accounts for over 60% of total NHS carbon emissions. Every supplier is part of the solution.
Over 2,000 suppliers have already registered. The NHS is supplied by over 80,000 companies. The race to differentiate has already begun.
Regulatory Basis
Health & Care Act 2022 · PPN 006 · PPN 002 · Climate Change Act 2008 · Environment Act 2021
📅 April 2026 — NOW
Level 1 Becomes the Minimum Gate
All NHS Supply Chain frameworks require Evergreen Level 1 as a minimum standard to continue doing business. The Patient Temperature Management 2027 framework (£150m) already enforces this.
📅 April 2027
Global Scope 3 CRPs Required
All suppliers must publish Carbon Reduction Plans covering global Scope 1, 2 and all Scope 3 emissions — effectively demanding Level 2+ capability. Suppliers at Level 1 face a critical 12-month window to bridge the gap.
📅 2030
Evergreen Progress = Hard Qualification
From 2030, suppliers will only qualify for NHS contracts by demonstrating Evergreen progress. Level 1 alone will not be sufficient to bid. Product-level carbon footprinting requirements follow from April 2028.
Maturity Framework

The Four Evergreen Levels — Explained

The framework is cumulative — each level builds on all requirements of the level below. Level 1 is now merely table stakes. Levels 2 and 3 are where competitive differentiation is won.

1
Publicly Committed
Table Stakes
  • Scope 1, 2 & partial Scope 3 (5 PPN 006 categories)
  • UK operations only
  • Net zero target by 2050 or sooner
  • Self-reported — no independent validation required
  • Legislative modern slavery compliance only
✓ Mandatory from April 2026
2
Comprehensive Targets
Competitive Edge
  • All 15 GHG Protocol Scope 3 categories (UK)
  • Modern slavery public statement required
  • Corporate social value programme required
  • Community, diversity & health/wellbeing programmes
  • Net zero by 2050 or sooner
⚡ Already demanded in live tenders
3
NHS-Aligned Targets
Strong Differentiator
  • Global operations — all Scope 3
  • Net zero by 2045 (NHS-aligned Carbon Footprint Plus)
  • Independent validation (e.g. SBTi)
  • Full MSAT completion — structured risk assessment
  • Board-level sustainability governance
⭐ Premium procurement positioning
4
Leadership Tier
Market Leader
  • Entity + parent company — all scopes globally
  • Recognised high performer (CDP, EcoVadis, or B Corp)
  • Active supply chain mapping & incident investigation

Market Reality

Level 1 Is Already Non-Competitive

The claim that suppliers can wait until April 2026 to engage with Evergreen is outdated. NHS procurement bodies are embedding Evergreen levels into live tenders right now — and the bar is rising fast.

Patient Temperature Management 2027 (£150m)

Published January 2026, this framework explicitly requires Evergreen Level 1 as a minimum. The tender notice was amended because the close date falls after 1 April 2026.

NHS England Medicines Tenders

A valid Evergreen submission has been a pass/fail condition of award since January 2024. NHS England explicitly encourages bidders to achieve the highest level possible — with annual KPI monitoring.

All NHS Supply Chain Frameworks

Evergreen completion has been required since February 2024 for all new and existing frameworks. Office Supplies 2026 pre-market engagement confirms Evergreen submission required for all bidders.

Social Value: 10–30% of Tender Score

Since April 2022, all NHS procurements include a minimum 10% net zero and social value weighting. ~80% of NOE CPC framework agreements apply at least this minimum.

Practical Steps

The Race to Level 2 Has Already Begun

Based on our experience supporting NHS suppliers, here is a realistic, costed view of what progression looks like — so you can plan with confidence.

Estimated 6–12 months

Level 1 → Level 2

Typical industry cost: £10,000–£50,000
  • Full Scope 3 Carbon Footprint — Screen all 15 GHG Protocol categories (not just the 5 required at Level 1). Estimated: £5,000–£25,000.
  • Corporate Social Value Programme — Community engagement, diversity & inclusion, volunteering, health/wellbeing aligned to PPN 002's Five Missions.
  • Modern Slavery Statement — Required at Level 2 even if below the £36m legislative threshold. Plus due diligence processes for supply chain risk.
  • Environmental Management System — ISO 14001 provides systematic infrastructure. Certification: ~£3,000–£10,000 in Year 1.
  • Supply Chain Engagement — Begin engaging Tier 1 suppliers on their own carbon reporting and sustainability expectations.
Estimated 12–24 months additional

Level 2 → Level 3

Typical industry cost: £30,000–£130,000+
  • Science-Based Targets (SBTi) — Commit, develop, and validate targets (near-term ~40 business days, net-zero ~60 days). SBTi costs: £1,980 (SMEs) to £16,731+.
  • Global Emissions Boundary — Expand from UK-only to all global facilities, operations, and subsidiaries. Cost: £5,000–£30,000.
  • 2045 Net Zero Target — Accelerate commitment to align with NHS Carbon Footprint Plus timeline, supported by a credible SBTi-validated reduction pathway.
  • Third-Party Verification — Independent emissions verification by SGS, Bureau Veritas, LRQA, or BSI. Cost: ~£5,000–£20,000.
  • Board-Level Governance — Appoint sustainability lead, integrate sustainability KPIs into executive performance frameworks, TCFD/UK SRS alignment.
